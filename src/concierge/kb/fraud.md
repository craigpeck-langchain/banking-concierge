# Reporting Fraud

## If you think your account has been compromised
1. Lock your debit and credit cards in the mobile app immediately
2. Change your online banking password and security questions
3. Call Meridian National:
   - Debit card fraud: 1-800-555-0100
   - Credit card fraud: 1-800-555-0142
   - Online or mobile banking fraud: 1-866-555-0168
4. Review recent activity and dispute any unrecognized transactions
5. Place a fraud alert with one of the three major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, or TransUnion); they're required to notify the others

## Zero Liability Protection
You're not liable for unauthorized transactions on your Meridian National consumer credit card or debit card when reported promptly. For debit cards, report within 60 days of the statement that lists the unauthorized transaction. After 60 days, federal Reg E protections may be reduced.

## If you've sent money to a scammer
Wire transfers, Zelle, and cashier's checks are generally not reversible. Call Meridian National right away (1-800-555-0100 for personal accounts) and file a police report. We will attempt to recall the transfer but cannot guarantee recovery.

## Common scams
- Impersonation calls or texts claiming to be from Meridian National Fraud
- "You have to move your money to a safe account" – Meridian National will never ask you to do this
- Romance and investment scams
- Job offers requiring you to deposit a check and forward funds

Meridian National will never call, text, or email asking you for your password, one-time passcode, ATM PIN, or to move money to a "safe" or "Meridian National" account.
