# Account Security

## Protecting your sign-on
- Use a strong, unique password for online banking. Don't reuse it for other sites.
- Turn on two-step verification under Security Center → Sign-on & Security.
- Use the Meridian National Mobile app's biometric sign-on (Face ID, Touch ID, fingerprint).
- Never share your username, password, one-time passcode, or PIN with anyone—including a "Meridian National agent" who calls you.

## Account alerts
Set up real-time alerts under Manage Alerts in online or mobile banking. Recommended:
- Sign-on from a new device
- Profile or password changes
- Large transactions (set a dollar threshold appropriate for you)
- Low balance
- Direct deposit posted

## Lock your cards
You can lock and unlock your debit and credit cards from the mobile app in seconds. Locking blocks new purchases and ATM withdrawals.

## Identity theft
If you suspect identity theft beyond your Meridian National accounts:
- Report at identitytheft.gov to receive a recovery plan
- Consider a fraud alert or credit freeze with all three credit bureaus
- File a police report and keep a copy of the case number

## Meridian National will never
- Ask you to move money to a "safe" or "Meridian National" account
- Ask for your password, one-time passcode, or PIN
- Ask you to send gift cards or cryptocurrency
- Pressure you to act immediately

If you receive any of these requests, hang up and call us back using the number on the back of your card or 1-800-555-0100.
