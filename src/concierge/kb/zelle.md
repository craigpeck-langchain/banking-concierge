# Zelle

## What is Zelle?
A fast, free way to send and receive money between U.S. bank accounts using only an email address or U.S. mobile number. Zelle is built into Meridian National online and mobile banking.

## Sending money
- Sign on → Transfer & Pay → Send Money with Zelle
- Enter the recipient's email or U.S. mobile number
- Enter the amount and a memo

If the recipient is already enrolled in Zelle, the money typically arrives in minutes. If they're not enrolled, they'll receive instructions; once they enroll, the payment completes.

## Limits
- $3,500 per day for sending from a personal Meridian National account
- $20,000 per 30-day period for sending

These are Meridian National's standard Zelle limits. Some accounts and senders may have lower limits based on risk and account history; you can see your current limits on the Send Money screen.

## Receiving money
There are no Meridian National limits on receiving Zelle payments to a Meridian National account.

## Reversal and disputes
Zelle is intended for sending money to people you know and trust. Once a Zelle payment is sent to an enrolled recipient, it is generally not reversible. If you sent money to the wrong person and they refuse to send it back, contact Meridian National to attempt a recovery, but recovery is not guaranteed.

If you authorized a Zelle payment as a result of a scam (impersonation, romance, investment), report it as soon as possible. Reimbursement rules under updated Zelle network policy may apply to certain impersonation scams—Meridian National will review case-by-case.
